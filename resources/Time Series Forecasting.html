Introduction

Time management is one of the most critical aspects of a student’s life. Efficiently managing classes, studies, and physical and mental health is significant for all-rounded success. This case study aims to understand essential aspects of everyday life and the differences among the students from the three analytics programs at Cambrian College: BAPG, CAGC, and HAGC. After this study, we should be able to provide answers to three key questions. Are there differences in the average study times for students in the different analytics streams? Is the distribution of days studied more than 3.13 hours (the average daily study time for students at McGill) the same for students in the different analytics streams? How does my study time change over time?

The data points for this study are a combination of quantitative and categorical characteristics. Semester (F22, W23) and Program (BAPG, CAGC, HAGC) are the categorical factors. The quantitative factor is the time spent studying (time in hours). Only the data from September 12, 2022, to April 5, 2023, is used in this study. The dataset’s level of granularity is day level.

Date, Study Hours, Semester, and Program are the variables I’ve chosen. I chose these variables because I wanted to understand if there is a change in my study hours across the fall and winter semesters and to analyze the differences in the study patterns of students from BAPG, HAGC, and CAGC programs.

Data Analysis

Part 1: ANOVA

Objective: Determine if the mean number of hours studied by students from the three programs: BAPG, CAGC, and HAGC are same or diferent.

Hypothesis:

\(H_0: \mu_B = \mu_C = \mu_H\)

\(H_A:\) At least one mean is different

# ANOVA summary
summary(anova_combined_50)
##              Df Sum Sq Mean Sq F value  Pr(>F)   
## Program       2   57.7  28.852   5.448 0.00522 **
## Residuals   147  778.5   5.296                   
## ---
## Signif. codes:  0 '***' 0.001 '**' 0.01 '*' 0.05 '.' 0.1 ' ' 1
# Calculate the critical F-value
qf(0.05, df1 = 2, df2 = 147, lower.tail=FALSE)
## [1] 3.057621

Since,

  • F-value = 5.448 > 3.057621 = critical f-value
  • p-value = 0.00522 < 0.05 = alpha

Decision: Reject the null hypothesis.

Inference: There is evidence that the mean number of hours studied in the three programs are different.

Check Conditions for ANOVA:

  • Independence Assumption: The experiment uses randomized data from the three programs. Study hours of students of one program does not affect the study hours of students of other programs.

  • Similar Variance Assumption: There are some differences specifically between BAPG and HAGC, but overall the variances are quite similar.

Part 2: Chi-Square Tests

Objective: Determine if there is evidence that the distribution of days with average study hours above 3.13 hours and average study hours below 3.13 hours is the same across programs.

Hypothesis:

\(H_0:\) Study hours and type of program are independent

\(H_A:\) Study hours and type of program are not independent

Chi-square statistics:
X-squared = 12.972
p-value = 0.001525
X^2* (critical value) = 5.991465
alpha = 0.05

Residuals:

Study Hours BAPG CAGC HAGC
Above -1.8569534 0.5570860 1.2998674
Below 2.1821789 -0.6546537 -1.5275252

Since,

  • x-squared = 12.972 > 5.991465 = X^2* or critical value
  • p-value = 0.001525 < 0.05 = alpha)

Decision: Reject the null hypothesis.

Inference: There is a difference in the above and below study hours across different program types.

Check conditions for chi-square test of independence

  • Counted Data Condition: Categories “Above” and “Below” are counted.
  • Independence Condition: Counts are independent since the data is randomly selected.
  • Randomization Condition: Samples randomly selected from the combined data for BAPG, HAGC, CAGC.
  • Sample Size Condition: 150 random samples > 5.

Analysis Summary:

Questions

  1. Are there differences in the average study times for students in the different analytics streams?

    Ans: Yes, there are differences in the average study times for students in the different analytics streams. The average daily study time for students in the CAGC stream is 4 hours and 5 minutes, followed by HAGC’s 3 hours and 49 minutes and BAPG’s 3 hours and 20 minutes.

    The ANOVA test supplemented with Tukey’s HSD confirmed that the mean number of hours studied in the three programs are different, and that the students in CAGC and HAGC programs spend significantly higher hours studying than students in BAPG program.

  2. Is the distribution of days studied more than 3.13 hours (the average daily study time for students at McGill) the same for students in the different analytics streams (or in other words, independent of program stream)?

    Ans: No, the distribution of days studied more than 3.13 hours is not the same for students in the different analytics streams. This indicate it is independent of program stream.

    The chi-square test of independence confirmed that there indeed is a difference in the above and below average study hours for the three programs. The standardized residuals of the chi-square analysis hinted that the below average study hours of the students from BAPG program was the most unusual.

  3. How does your personal study time change over time?

    Ans: My study hours have declined from 4 hours 28 minutes in the Fall semester to 3 hours and 50 minutes in the Winter semester. This change was a result of several factors. I started working part-time, cooked my meals instead of eating out, and joined the cricket team at Cambrian College for the winter semester. Additionally, because of all the experience gained from the previous semester that helped me better manage my college commitments, navigating the winter semester was more manageable than in the fall. The average number of hours needed to keep up with the college decreased due to these modifications.
